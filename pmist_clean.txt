Faculty of Architecture and Planning
" The Faculty of Architecture and Planning offers over 30 years of excellence in architectural education, emphasizing creativity, sustainability, and interdisciplinary collaboration. With a focus on real-world learning and alignment with UN SDGs, we prepare students to address global design challenges innovatively."
Welcome to the Faculty of Architecture and Planning! As we celebrate over 30 years of excellence in architectural education, I am delighted to connect with our dynamic community of students, faculty, alumni, and partners who contribute to our mission of advancing architectural and planning education.
Our faculty is committed to fostering a learning environment where creativity, critical thinking, and sustainability are at the heart of everything we do. We offer a well-rounded education that combines rigorous study with hands-on, practical experience, integrating modern practices with the insights of traditional design wisdom. Our mission is to prepare future professionals to meet societys evolving needs with resilience, vision, and purpose.
With a strong emphasis on interdisciplinary collaboration, we provide students with opportunities to work alongside peers in engineering, technology, and management, encouraging holistic approaches to complex design challenges.
Our curriculum prepares students to succeed in multidisciplinary domains from product design to large-scale regional planning-equipping them with versatile skills that enable them to address diverse design problems innovatively.
Aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), our programs address critical issues such as climate change, global warming, disaster resilience, and sustainability across scales, from micro to macro levels. Additionally, we emphasize 'earn while you learn' opportunities, giving students real-world exposure to professional practice while they study. Lifelong learning is a cornerstone of our approach, with our faculty and alumni mentors providing guidance and support as students transition from the classroom to impactful careers.
We invite you to explore our website to learn more about our programs and initiatives, and we welcome you to join us on this journey of sustainable design, interdisciplinary exploration, and societal impact.

PMIST is using home grown software named BRAIN (Brilliant Resource Academic Institution) to manage the institution activities for two decades. A dedicated team of programmers are appointed to develop the software. This ERP is developed with ASP.Net as front end and SQL Server as Back end. It has the following modules
PMIST E-Governance Manual View
It facilitates student's admission. It captures details including personal information, marks details, fees payment and other relevant information for admission.
Every year students are given with scholarship, this module helps to store all the scholarship details and enables the scholarship allotment for the students. Also this module takes care of the scholarship renewal.
It provides facility for storing all Outcome Based Education related information like Department Vision, Mission, PEO, PO, PSO, curriculum entry, course entry etc.
This module enables the faculty members to enter the course plan details, student's attendance and students' marks for the course which they handle.
This module captures details of all books purchased for the library. Also it takes care of the students/staff book borrowing and book return.
All exam related activities like, fees payment for exams, hall ticket generation, hall plan generation, marks entry, grade generation and result preparation are done by using this module.
It helps the warden to manage the rooms availability in the hostel. Room allotment is made when a student is admitted in the hostel after fees payment. Also the students can apply for an out pass using this module whenever they want to go out of the hostel.
It helps the transport in-charge to manage the bus details and route details. Students are assigned for the specific route bus by using this module
Provision for fees posting management is done using this module. All fees payments, including online payment are performed by using this module.
This module facilitates the faculty members to upload the e-contents developed by them. Students can view the e-contents uploaded by the faculty members through the intranet facility.
Whenever a staff is inducted in the Institution the details including their personal information and their salary band are captured using this module.
This module is for the staff to apply leave, OD, and permission to the relevant authorities. Authorities in turn approve the same.
Staff attendance is captured using the punching system installed in the campus. Based on the presence and leave details salary is calculated for each staff.
It is used for storing all the visitors/vehicle entering the campus.

Welcome to the Faculty of Computing Science and Applications (FCSA) at Periyar Maniammai Institute of Science and Technology (Deemed to be University).
The field of Computer Science is central to the development of advanced technology, and at our faculty, we offer a diverse curriculum that is carefully crafted and regularly updated to align with the ever evolving demands of the IT industry. Our mission is to cultivate confident individuals equipped with the skills needed for successful careers in the industry, empowering them to make meaningful contributions to society.
The Faculty of Computing Science and Applications offers an exciting suite of six undergraduate programs: BCA, B.Sc. Computer Science, B.Sc. Artificial Intelligence, B.Sc. Computer Science (Cyber Security), B.Sc. Animation & Multimedia, and B.Sc. Data Science. Additionally, we provide three postgraduate programs MCA, M.Sc. Computer Science, and M.Sc. Data Science & along with a Ph.D. program in Computer Science and Applications.
To support our students growth, we offer state-of-the-art infrastructure, including modern computer labs equipped with advanced technology. Our faculty members are highly qualified, with extensive academic and industrial experience in their fields, fostering a professional learning environment that ensures students receive the highest quality education.
Beyond academic excellence, we focus on preparing students for career success through industry internships, certification programs, and hands-on workshops that provide practical experience and industry insights. With the support of a dedicated team of staff and a distinguished alumni network of over 3,000 members, our institution benefits from a community of scholars, innovators, and leaders who have made a lasting impact on both our institution and society.
The Faculty of Computing Science and Applications is dedicated to delivering a thorough and rewarding educational experience, empowering students with the knowledge and skills essential for success in the ever-evolving field of computer science.

Dr. K. Veeramani stands as a beacon of social reform, education, and community service, embodying the ideals of leadership, innovation, and unwavering dedication to societal progress. Inspired by the philosophy of Thanthai Periyar, he has relentlessly pursued the goals of social justice, education, and empowerment, reshaping institutions and transforming lives.
Born: December 2, 1933, Cuddalore Old Town, Tamil Nadu, India
Spouse: Thirumathi Mohana (Inter-caste marriage in 1958)
Children: Two sons and two daughters

Editor of Viduthalai since 1978, shaping public discourse through his writings and speeches.
Over six decades of addressing social issues globally through conferences, seminars, and public platforms.

His enduring legacy inspires millions to strive for a more just and equitable world.
Dr. Veeramani has represented Indian rationalist and humanist ideals on international platforms, participating in conferences in the USA, Canada, Europe, and Asia.
His lectures, including the G.U. Pope Memorial Lecture at the House of Lords, London, have inspired global audiences.

Founded Periyar Organization for Women Empowerment and Renaissance (POWER) and established free clinics, schools, and community colleges across Tamil Nadu.
Recipient of the First International Award - "CIDA - Award of Excellence" - awarded by Canadian Government to Periyar Centenary Polytechnic College for its effective implementation of Community Development Scheme.

STUDENT COUNCIL ELECTION PROCEDURE
Undergraduates between ages 17 and 24 can contest. Although no academic criteria have been prescribed for the candidates to contest, however, they should not have any academic arrears in their election year. The candidates must be elected as a class representative by his/her class students. Candidates should have the minimum attendance of 80 percent. Candidates will not have criminal records i.e. they should not have been tried and/or convicted of any criminal offence or misdemeanour. They would also not have been subject to any disciplinary action by PMIST authorities. Candidates must be regular, full time students. The candidates violating this rule will be disqualified.
Class Representative Election Procedure
The election officer/Class in-charge can conduct the election. On the date of election, election officer shall go to the class room and ask for the nominations to be made on the spot. The election officer has to make sure the eligibility condition of the candidates and then writes the names of the contestants serially on the black board. Ballot papers are signed by the election officer and will be issued to the students. After that the election officers have to ask the voters to cast the vote. The voters have to write only one candidate's name on the ballot paper. After all the voters present in the room have cast their votes, the election officer asks them to settle down quietly while the ballots are taken out and tallied. If a candidate has obtained maximum votes, he/she is declared as the winner.
As per the J.M.Lyngdoh committee recommendations, the direct elections to be conducted in Periyar Maniammai Institute of Science & Technology.
Election is to be held annually and will be conducted within six weeks from the start of the academic session. The entire election process, from the date of filing nomination papers, campaigning and declaration of the results, should not exceed 10 days.
No candidate will indulge in, nor abet, any activity, which may aggravate existing differences or cause tension between different castes and communities, religious or linguistic groups or groups of students. Criticism of other candidates will be confined to their policies and programmes, past record and work. Contestants will refrain from criticism of all aspects of private life not of other candidates. There will be no appeal to caste or communal feelings for votes and places of worship will not be used for electoral propaganda. No printed posters, pamphlets will be allowed and only hand-made material will be permitted at places notified by the authorities. Defacement or destruction of property of the university will not be permitted.
